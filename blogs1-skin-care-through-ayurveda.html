Skin care through Ayurveda

Ayurveda is the ancient holistic system of healing. Skin disorders are predominantly explained in the ayurvedic text of charak and sushrut samhita. Ayurvedic skin care treatments are based on skin type. According to Ayurveda, a personâ€™s skin type is based on three doshas and sapta dhatu. These are bioenergetic or life forces that make up the constitution of the body and mind. They are:

  • Vata Dosha (wind)
  • Pitta Dosha (fire)
  • Kapha Dosha (water and earth)

Pitta dosha predominantly resides in your skin and liver. Pitta dosha along with other doshas decides the texture and colour of your skin. Liver is the seat for pitta dosha, hence anything happen in liver has a twin effect on your skin.

Ayurveda mainly focuses on life style and dietary changes in daily routine (i.e. pathya and apthya), medicine comes on third priority. This holistic system believes in Cleaning (Shodhana) of vitiated doshas and healing from the root level.

Skin disorders generally aggravated due to improper and unhealthy dietary habits. Ayurveda strictly opposes potency incompatibility (Veerya viruddha) i.e. hot food over cold food item or vice versa, because this disturbs the normal digestive process and metabolism of your gut, that directly has a negative impact on your liver. Food items rich in saturated fat, cheese, uneven spices have negative impact as well.

Another thing you need to worry about is acidity. Long lasting acidity can cause acne and infective skin disorders. Improper secretions from your gut can impact the health of liver hence vitiate pitta dosha and cause skin problems.

Before using any skin related remedy or treatment, you must be aware of your skin type, because the remedy that is beneficial for others, may or may not be good for your skin type. Every human doesn't have same skin type. Combination or mixture of three dosha decides your skin type. The quantity of dosha decides quality of skin. like if a person's skin is having vata dosha in more quantity than pitta and kapha, it will be dry and may or may not posses cracks and numbness, because vata dosha is mase up of wind (vayu). Similarly if a person is having pitta dosha in more quantity, it will initiate symptoms of burning, wounds, inflammation and infection like symptoms.

Change in daily routine and dietary habits can give you better results along with medication. breakfast before 8 in morning, lunch before 12 in noon, and dinner before 7 in evening is a good dietary habit to follow. Eating less than appetite can bring a great positive shift in skin related problems.

Protein rich peanuts, chickpea along with carbohydrate rich jaggery and milk is beneficial for morning breakfast.

Eat less sprouts and green leafy vegetables as it can cause acidity, we should eat according to the habitat where we live, you can prefer eating Lauki (bottle-gourd), Karela (Bitter-gourd), kaddu (Pumpkin). You must take 100 ml of Chhach (butter milk) after having lunch as it is best for digestion. Intake of tea and coffee must be reduced while facing skin related issues. Dinner must be taken before 7 in evening and it must be less than the appetite, light, less spicy and liquid in nature.

Bhrungraj, Neem, Giloy, Sariva can miraculously benefit in skin disorders. Neem decoction (kada) 50 ml must be taken empty stomach in the morning, giloy swaras (juice) 20 ml in morning is also a good choice. fresh amla achar (Pickel) should be taken with meal. All these medicine should be taken under the supervision of qualified ayurvedic physician.
